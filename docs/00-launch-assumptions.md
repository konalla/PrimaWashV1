# Launch assumptions and open decisions

## Working assumptions

These assumptions allow product and engineering work to begin without silently turning unknowns into permanent architecture.

- The launch wedge is Singapore residential vehicle care, led by condominium activation and Prima Wash Days.
- Condo-first does not mean condo-only; HDB and landed-property customers remain supported through trusted nearby care.
- The customer product is a native iOS and Android application.
- Partners and internal operators use a responsive web application.
- One country, currency, and time-zone region launch first, while core architecture remains market-scoped and global-ready.
- A partner may own multiple locations; staff access is scoped to an organization and optionally a location.
- Prima Wash facilitates booking and payment, but payment custody and partner payout depend on the selected provider and jurisdiction.
- Customers may reschedule or cancel according to a versioned policy captured on each booking.
- Memberships follow the residential and condo-operations foundation because repeat usage is strategically important.
- Fleet management, broad marketplace expansion, and AI-assisted transactions follow the core residential booking release.
- Most condos do not have dedicated wash bays; the platform must support temporary, management-approved operating areas, water-efficient services, and property-specific rules.
- Pickup/return may be needed when on-site service is not practical or permitted.
- CLIQSA validates MVP operations but must not constrain the native Prima Wash platform architecture.

## Decisions required from product leadership

1. Define CLIQSA and how it currently validates demand without shaping long-term workflows.
2. Confirm Singapore, SGD, and Asia/Singapore as the first market settings.
3. Confirm the first service categories and which are allowed for condo on-site, pickup/return, dropoff, and mobile dispatch.
4. Decide whether partners or Prima Wash control pricing by market mode and fulfilment mode.
5. Define cancellation, no-show, refund, dispute, vehicle movement, and pickup/return liability policies.
6. Decide whether memberships are platform-wide, property-specific, partner-specific, or hybrid.
7. Define the initial commercial model: commission, subscription, lead fee, residence partnership, or hybrid.
8. Define condo activation statuses and minimum criteria for an active Prima Wash Day.
9. Define what customer/property/partner communications are in-product at MVP versus handled operationally.

Each resolved decision should be recorded as an architecture or product decision record.
